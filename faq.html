Frequently Asked Questions for Supervised Students

This FAQ is based on my experience supervising thesis students in different countries and heavily inspired by my colleague Daniel Graziotin. Since it’s often a student’s first time approaching a possible supervisor, they can’t be expected to know these answers. This document helps streamline my supervision process and avoids repeating information for every new request.

Being familiar with this FAQ increases your chances of having me as your supervisor and clarifies expectations for your thesis work. It mainly applies to Master’s students, though Bachelor’s students may also find it useful.

If you contact me for supervision, mention in your email that you’ve read this FAQ.

First Contact

  • Are you accepting students for supervision?
    • Usually, yes. Bachelor students can check with the course coordinator. Master’s students should refer to my available slots on Canvas.
  • Do I need to be a former student of yours to be supervised?
    • No, I value motivation, creativity, and independence.
  • Do I need excellent grades to have you supervise my thesis?
    • No, motivation and initiative matter more than grades.
  • How should I approach you for a thesis project?
    • Email me with an idea related to my research interests (check ‘Potential Supervisors’ on Canvas for Master’s). Also, mention that you’ve read this FAQ.
  • When should I contact you about supervision?
    • At least a month before you plan to start, earlier is even better, especially for Master’s projects. This allows us to exchange ideas and assess if we’re a good fit.

Topics

  • Can my thesis be about developing a software tool/system?
    • You can develop software for your thesis, but the software itself is not the thesis.
  • Can a company-sponsored tool development be my thesis?
    • No. A thesis must involve research, even if it’s company-related. There must be empirical validation of the tool’s impact.
  • Will you sign an NDA if my company requires it?
    • Yes, but the thesis must be publicly available. We can anonymize it and get company approval before publication.
  • How do I find thesis topics related to your research?
    • Check my suggested topics on Canvas and look at my published papers.
  • How should I propose a thesis idea?
    • No formal proposal needed just a one-pager with research questions, justification based on existing literature, and a preliminary methodology.

Communication & Meetings

  • Do we have to communicate in English?
    • Yes.
  • Will we have regular meetings?
    • Meetings are flexible based on need, not fixed schedules. I have limited supervision hours (33 for MSc, 25 for BSc). If your question is brief, email instead.
  • Do you avoid meetings?
    • No. I support students and provide feedback. Just come prepared with work to discuss.
  • Who manages the meetings?
    • You do. When requesting a meeting, propose an agenda. During the meeting, drive the discussion and summarize your progress.

Feedback Mechanisms

  • When should I contact you to arrange a meeting?
    • At least 10 days in advance.
  • When should I send work for feedback?
    • At least two weeks before the meeting, so I have time to review it.
  • I want to keep working on my thesis until the last minute before the meeting!
    • Send me a version in advance for feedback. You can continue refining it afterward.
  • I’m embarrassed to show my early drafts.
    • Every great paper starts as a rough draft. Get feedback early and often it leads to better results.
  • Will my early drafts affect my grade?
    • No. I focus on the final version.
  • Can I send my final thesis the day before submission for approval?
    • No. I need at least a week to review it.

Writing Your Thesis

  • Which template should I use?
    • Check Canvas.
  • Can I write my thesis in Microsoft Word or LibreOffice?
    • No. Use LaTeX.
  • Do I really have to use LaTeX?
    • Yes. I also recommend BibTeX for citations.
  • But LaTeX is hard!
    • It’s not, especially for academic writing. Overleaf provides an easy-to-use interface, and as a BTH student, you have premium access.
  • How do I format citations and references?
    • Use the template, it handles formatting for you.
  • How do I cite websites, videos, or talks?
    • Use a BibTeX entry and include the access date.
  • Will you teach me how to write my thesis?
    • No, but I will support you.
  • Do you have any other tips about LaTeX?
    • Check Robert Dale's article.
  • Can I read past theses?
    • Some are available in the university library and through fellow students.
  • How long should my thesis be?
    • As long as needed. Master’s theses typically range from 35–90 pages.
  • What is plagiarism and how do I avoid it?
    • Read https://plagiarism.org.

Learning Resources

I assume you’re familiar with basic research methods and will consult the following before asking questions.

  • Research Methods:

    • Shull, F., Singer, J., & Sjøberg, D. I. K. (2008). Guide to Advanced Empirical Software Engineering.
    • Wohlin, C., et al. (2012). Experimentation in Software Engineering.
    • Felderer, M., & Travassos, G. H. (2020). Contemporary Empirical Methods in Software Engineering.

  • Thesis Writing:

    • Hofmann, A. H. (2019). Scientific Writing and Communication.
    • Booth, W. C., et al. (2016). The Craft of Research.

Grading

  • How do I get a good grade?
    • Follow the project description and deliver a clear and well-written thesis.
  • What if my chosen approach doesn’t work?
    • That’s part of research. Document your findings.
  • What if I don’t complete everything in the project description?
    • If you can’t do something, discuss alternatives with me.
  • What if I run out of time?
    • Check the course regulations for your options.
  • Where is my deadline?
    • On Canvas.

Common Concerns

  • Will you micromanage me?
    • No. I expect independence and initiative.
  • What if I don’t know what to do next?
    • Propose a next step. Any idea is better than none.
  • I need someone to tell me exactly what to do!
    • I’m not the right supervisor for you.
  • Do you intentionally fail students?
    • No. I want every student to succeed.
  • Is there a quota for failing students?
    • No, that’s a myth.
  • Why are your emails so short? Are you mad at me?
    • No. I prefer brief, direct communication.
  • Can I call you or visit your office unannounced?
    • No, email me instead.
  • Can I add you on WhatsApp/Signal/Discord?
    • No.
